Overview

Purpose
The portfolio showcases the depth and breadth of knowledge and skills gained during the Education Specialist (Ed.S.) in Instructional Technology program at Kennesaw State University. It serves as evidence of my ability to integrate technology into instructional design and demonstrates my readiness for leadership roles in educational technology.

Objective
The objective was to document and reflect on completed coursework, practical applications, and professional growth. It also highlights key projects, certifications, and achievements aligned with instructional technology standards.

Role
As the creator, I assumed the role of both designer and curator, responsible for selecting and presenting artifacts that represent my competencies and growth as an educational technology specialist.

Duration
The portfolio was developed over the course of the program, 2 years, with periodic updates to include new projects and reflections.

Process

  1. Planning and Organization
    Outlined key sections.
    Reviewed program requirements and instructional technology standards to align content.
  2. Artifact Selection
    Identified significant coursework, projects, and professional experiences.
    Included reflective components to articulate lessons learned and their impact on practice.
  3. Design and Development
    Created the portfolio using Weebly.
    Applied design principles for clarity, accessibility, and professional aesthetics.
    Integrated multimedia elements to enhance engagement.
  4. Reflection and Iteration
    Wrote reflective summaries for each artifact, connecting them to program outcomes.
  5. Finalization and Presentation
    Conducted a comprehensive review to ensure all requirements were met.
    Published the portfolio online, ensuring accessibility and professional appearance.
    Presented the portfolio to faculty and peers as part of the program's capstone requirement.
